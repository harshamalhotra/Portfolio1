Nom Nom

Nom Nom is an iPhone application for people to order food from local food trucks. It allows searching food trucks by location, viewing menus, adding and viewing reviews, selecting and ordering food, and completing the payment.

To conduct my research for Nom Nom, I interviewed 20 persons to understand what they were looking in a food truck experience. This helped me understand my potential users and their pain points in ordering food from the food trucks. Some of my findings include:

  • Users should be able to search for food trucks in any area
  • They should have a way to track some of their favorite food trucks
  • Users should be able to place an order remotely using the app so that they can only go to pick up their food and minimize their waiting time
  • People should be able to pay digitally using the app itself

Little Hope

Little hope is a responsive web application that enables people to donate to and organize crowdfunding campaigns for children related causes.

While I was designing low fidelity wireframes for Little Hope, I had to answer an important product question — which out of “Donate” and “Start a project” should be the primary call-to-action.

To answer this question, I went through my research notes again and saw that people who were creating fundraisers were inherently motivated and ready to spend time and energy to build a project. I also concluded that the action of donation on the other hand was much lighter weight and people were more likely to drop out and not complete the process. Based on this, I decided that the primary call-to-action would be for donation with a goal of making the donation process as easy as possible.

After conducting a series of usability tests, I found out that my users found the process of donation to be easy but the process of building a project seemed too take too long with a lot of information to fill-in. This was another challenge that I wanted to solve.

Baksa

Baksa is a responsive SaaS web application allowing users to collect and store their documents, images and links.

After conducting competitive analysis and interviews for baksa , it was clear that my potential user base had different and sometimes competing requirements. For instance, some power users cared about writing documents whereas most others cared about storing images. So I crafted user personas based on my research to ensure that I was designing for all the potential users.
